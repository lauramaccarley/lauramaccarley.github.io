  • Year in awards

    It’s hard to be humble.

    But we finally got our founder the recognition he deserves.

    Craig Young was named Volunteer of the Year on National Philanthropy Day and was recognized in the Business Courier’s Second Act awards.

    We were named partner of the year by OneSource, were named a finalist in Independent Sector’s and Accenture’s Innovate for Good Challenge.

  • Year in board connect

    Creating WIDER nonprofit boards

    Welcoming, Inclusive, Diverse, and Representative

    in Greater Cincinnati! We’ve tracked more than 30 volunteer leaders in our region who have used BoardConnect, formerly BoardBank, to find a way to help on nonprofit boards and committees. Many of these connections occurred because of two connecting events -- one in May and one in October. To help fill the gap in knowledge about how to serve on a nonprofit board, we’ve launched a digital board bootcamp series, available to the general public digitally as well as in person at businesses on demand.

    Check it out!
  • Year in service enterprise

    We’re walking our own talk

    by becoming a certified Service Enterprise

    But even better -- we were named by the national certification organization, the Points of Light Foundation, as the Ohio/Northern Kentucky/ Southeast Indiana hub for leading nonprofits.

    Our first cohort began in the fall, including Valley View, YMCA, the Literacy Network of Greater Cincinnati, Freestore Foodbank, Cincinnati Parks and the Cincinnati Museum Center. Look for these organizations to be certified soon, and our second cohort to start in 2020.

  • Year in monthly reports

    Hello, it’s good to know you!

    We featured LADD in our August nonprofit report.

    You’ve gotten to know us through our monthly reports, a different newsletter each month for individuals, nonprofits and businesses, telling and sharing the stories of greater volunteer engagement in our community and elsewhere.

  • Year in national involvement

    The frequent flyer miles are racking up.

    There was a time this past fall where our founder, Craig Young, was in five different cities across four weeks connecting, attending and volunteering in a variety of national initiatives.

    Craig is a founding member of the National Alliance for Volunteer Engagement, and continues to be deeply embedded in the national volunteer ecosystem through the Points of Light Foundation, the Independent Sector, the Association for Research on Nonprofit Organizations and the Voluntary Sector, Tech Impact and the Encore Network, among several others.

  • Year in other cities

    From East to West, poor to rich.

    Other communities are seeing what we’re doing in Cincinnati, and are asking for our solutions to their biggest volunteer ecosystem challenges. Our platform is at work across the entire state of Nevada, is at work in Boston and in southeast Ohio/ Northeast Kentucky. Beginning next year, residents of the city of Los Angeles will use our platform to begin discovering their ways to help.

  • Year in team members

    It’s all about our team!

    From our graphic designer, Zoe Storch, to our sales leader, Beth Guzior, to our new chief impact officer, Vanessa Mosely to our chief national community development officer, Tim Thomas -- and then a full complement of volunteers, we’re blessed to be working in an environment that is so rewarding and impactful.

  • Year in funding

    "Money is like manure.”

    “It’s not worth a thing until it’s spread around encouraging young things to grow.”

    We’re a young thing (founded in April 2017), and we’re definitely growing, thanks to generous supporters.

    Our work has been made possible by a number of generous individuals and foundations, including the Craig Young Family Foundation, the Greater Cincinnati Foundation, Lee and Shannon Carter, Nevada Volunteers, the Adams County Medical Foundation and several generous individuals who helped us win the Cincinnati Gives challenge hosted by Cincinnati Magazine and presented by Procter & Gamble Co.
